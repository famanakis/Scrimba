Hikes I Recommend

Secret Falls

Kauai - Summer 2020

11 comments

The Secret Falls hike in Kauai is a unique and historical trail that takes visitors through the lush jungle and along the Wailua River. The trail is approximately 2 miles long and starts at the Wailua River State Park, leading hikers through the remnants of an ancient Hawaiian village. Along the way, visitors can see several ancient structures, including a heiau (Hawaiian temple) and the remains of traditional Hawaiian homes. The highlight of the hike is Secret Falls, a beautiful waterfall that can be accessed via a short side trail. Visitors can swim in the refreshing pool at the base of the falls and enjoy the stunning scenery.

The Hawaiian Village and Secret Falls hike is a popular destination for visitors to Kauai who are interested in history and culture. The trail is relatively easy, with only a few short, steep sections, making it accessible to hikers of all skill levels. The area is also popular for kayaking and canoeing, as visitors can paddle up the Wailua River to access the trailhead. Overall, the Secret Falls hike is a great way to experience the natural beauty and cultural history of Kauai in one unique adventure.

Wind Caves

Cache National Forest - Spring 2019

7 comments

The Wind Caves hike in Cache National Forest is a scenic and relatively easy trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is approximately 3.5 miles round trip and is perfect for hikers of all skill levels. The highlight of the hike is the Wind Caves, a series of natural caves and tunnels that have been eroded into the sandstone cliffs. Visitors can explore the caves and enjoy the spectacular views from the top of the cliffs.

The trailhead for the Wind Caves hike is located just outside the small town of Logan, Utah. The hike itself is well-marked and easy to follow, with only a few steep sections that require a bit of scrambling. The area is also popular for mountain biking and horseback riding, so visitors may encounter other outdoor enthusiasts on the trail. Overall, the Wind Caves hike is a great way to explore the natural beauty of the Cache National Forest and experience the thrill of exploring natural caves and tunnels.

Heart of the Rocks Loop

Chiricahua National Monument - Fall 2017

3 comments

The Heart of the Rocks Hike is a challenging and rewarding trail located in the Chiricahua National Monument in southeastern Arizona. The trail is a 7.3-mile loop that takes hikers through some of the most spectacular rock formations in the park. Along the way, you'll see towering spires, balancing rocks, and eroded pinnacles that have been shaped by millions of years of weathering and erosion. The trail also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including the distant peaks of the Chiricahua Mountains.

The Heart of the Rocks Hike is rated as strenuous and requires a good level of fitness and experience. The trail is steep in places and includes some scrambling over rocks and boulders. However, the effort is well worth it as the views and scenery are truly breathtaking. Be sure to bring plenty of water, snacks, and sunscreen, as the trail can be exposed to the sun in some places. Overall, the Heart of the Rocks Hike is an unforgettable experience that is sure to be a highlight of any visit to the Chiricahua National Monument.

Havasupai Falls

Grand Canyon - Summer 2015

6 comments

The Havasupai Falls hike is a stunning adventure that takes you through the breathtaking landscape of the Grand Canyon. The hike to the falls is a 10-mile trek that starts at the Hualapai Hilltop and descends down into the Havasu Canyon. The hike offers spectacular views of the canyon, the blue-green waters of the Havasu Creek, and the lush vegetation of the surrounding canyon walls. The trail can be challenging, especially on the way back up, so it's important to prepare adequately and bring enough water and snacks to keep you energized.

Once you reach the Havasupai Falls, you'll be rewarded with an unforgettable sight. The falls consist of a series of cascading waterfalls that flow into the turquoise pools below. The water is cool and refreshing, making it the perfect place to take a dip and cool off after the long hike. Camping is available in the area, allowing visitors to spend the night and explore the falls further. Overall, the Havasupai Falls hike is a must-do for anyone who loves nature and wants to experience the beauty of the Grand Canyon up close.
